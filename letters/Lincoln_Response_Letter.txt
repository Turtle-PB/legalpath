  A.  29 CFR 2560.503-1(i) — Timeliness of Appeal Decision

Under 29 CFR 2560.503-1(i)(1)(i), a plan administrator must notify a claimant of the benefit determination on review "within a reasonable period of time, but not later than 60 days after receipt of the claimant's request for review." Under 29 CFR 2560.503-1(i)(3)(i), the administrator may extend this period by up to 60 additional days if "special circumstances" require, provided the administrator notifies the claimant of the extension and the circumstances requiring it.

The maximum time permitted under ERISA for an appeal decision is therefore 120 days. Lincoln took approximately 1,180 days — nearly ten (10) times the maximum permitted period. This is a per se procedural violation of ERISA claims procedure regulations.

  B.  29 CFR 2560.503-1(l) — Deemed Exhaustion

Under 29 CFR 2560.503-1(l)(1)(i), if a plan administrator fails to strictly adhere to the claims procedure requirements of 29 CFR 2560.503-1, the claimant is deemed to have exhausted the administrative remedies available under the plan. This means the claimant may immediately pursue remedies under ERISA Section 502(a) (29 U.S.C. 1132(a)).

Lincoln's failure to issue a timely appeal decision under 29 CFR 2560.503-1(i) constitutes a failure to strictly adhere to the claims procedure requirements.
